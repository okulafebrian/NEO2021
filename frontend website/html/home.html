ABOUT NEO

National English Olympics (NEO) is an annual event presented by Bina Nusantara English Club (BNEC) which aims to gather the potential of talents from students around Indonesia. This year’s NEO consists of 5 competitions, namely Debate, Newscasting, Podcast, Short Story Writing, and Speech.

ABOUT NATIONAL ENGLISH OLYMPICS

Theme

Luminescence

Luminescence is the emission of lights with a long radiation wavelength but lower energy than any light. Even though these lights have lower energy, they would still shine as brightly as they can in the darkest places. With the darkness lighted, the brightest light can be seen.

CONCEPT

In this time, covid-19 has pervaded our society and constrained our activity. People are compelled to distance themselves from others, have their activity suspended or delayed. The concept “The glory of my glow” carries a meaning of empowering people to be more courageous in displaying their talent, whatever it is, and have confidence in it.

BINUS ENGLISH CLUB

The Organizer

BNEC is the best organization at Binus University. We are a student-led organization English Club for language learners to use English in a casual setting. BNEC is also known as Bina Nusantara English club that was established on June 11th, 1992.

BNEC provides a community in which those who have a passion for learning English may be able to meet and connect through various events and experiences provided by the organization! Last but not least, BNEC has external events and one of the external events is the National English Olympics.

BINUS UNIVERSITY

The Host Campus

Bina Nusantara University is a university located in Jakarta, Indonesia which was awarded "The Best Private University" in the August 2013 issue of MIX magazine. BINUS University has many academic programs that aim to ensure the readiness of Bina Nusantara University students to work at national and international levels. In addition, BINUS University has more than 1500 professors, associate professors, and members of the teaching staff, who are experienced in academic practitioners with master (S2) and Ph.D. (S3) educational backgrounds to ensure the quality of education at Bina Nusantara University.

About this competition

Critical thinking, broad-mindedness, persuasiveness, and argumentative are things that are needed in debate to defend the argument and will enhance your bravery, critical, logical, and agile ways of thinking. Participating in a debate competition will help you to upgrade those skills, and now we are here to announce to you that the National English Olympics will hold a debate competition with the British Parliamentary System. So don't hesitate to sign up now!

General Rules
  1. The Zoom link for the competition will be sent to the participants via email and WhatsApp group one day before the D-Day of the competition round.
  2. The Debate competition will use the British Parliamentary System. Each team will consist of 2 debaters.
  3. Participants must use English during the competition. The speech must not conduct discrimination against SARA nor contain graphic sexual content. Participants must not include any form of profanity.
  4. Participants must attend the Technical Meeting and highly recommend attending the Coaching Clinic to learn some tips and tricks from the debate adjudicator.
  5. Participants who don’t attend the Technical Meeting and Coaching Clinic won't be guaranteed to receive further information from the committee after those sessions.
  6. Before each round starts, there will be a Technical Check to ensure that participants' mic and camera are functioning (audio must be clear and without echo, static, or other excessive noise). The camera must be stable to prevent unwanted movement.
  7. There will be a roll call before every round starts. All participants will be called a maximum of three times before being considered as a walk-out or disqualified.
  8. Every participant should standby in the chatroom 30 minutes before their performance. The participants must consent the performance to be recorded to avoid any misjudgment from a bad connection.
  9. The participants’ facial expressions must be visible throughout the debate.
  10. Participants are highly recommended to use laptops or smartphones to prevent sudden disconnection if a blackout happens during the performance.
  11. Participants are highly recommended to have a backup internet connection, such as mobile data or other wifi connections. If the participants experience unstable connections in the competition, it is not the responsibility of the NEO 2021 committee.
  12. Participants must wear semi-formal or formal clothes.
  13. Only the single-camera setup is permitted; the use of multiple camera angles is prohibited.
  14. The camera must have an unobstructed, focused view of the participants. The camera should receive enough lighting, not too bright or dark. Make sure the lighting is pointed to the debaters, not the camera.
  15. Zooming in or out is prohibited when performing.
  16. All adjudicators’ decisions and results are final.
Technical Rules
  1. All participants must join the waiting room at least 30 minutes before the round starts.
  2. Draw Release will be published on the tab page and WhatsApp group, and debaters will receive a Zoom link in the tab page and WhatsApp group.
  3. After all of the participants have been informed about their draws and positions, they must rename their Zoom display name with the format:
    • Round_Room_Position_Name
    • Example: R1_A5_CO_James
  4. A team is allowed to use one device for the two members to perform; just change the Zoom display name with the format:
    • Round_Room_Position_Name & Name
    • Example: R3_A2_CO_James & Dina
  5. The motion will be released exactly 15 minutes after the draw release through Zoom and WhatsApp.
  6. The committee will still execute the motion release even though some participants are not in the meeting yet. Please always check the WhatsApp group or tab page for motions.
  7. Debaters will be given 15 minutes for case building after the motion is released.
  8. When the motion is released, breakout rooms (the debate room) will be opened. Then, participants will be assigned to their predetermined debate room.
  9. If debater(s) are not present when the debate starts, all debaters in the room will wait for the missing debater(s) for 2 minutes while the committee is contacting the missing debaters in the WhatsApp group. If the missing debater(s) are still not present after 2 minutes, the committee will send a swing team to the room. The missing debater(s) will be dropped for that round and allocated to the next round.
  10. If debater(s) suddenly get disconnected when debating, all the debater(s) in the room will wait for 2 minutes for them to reconnect. If the whole team gets disconnected, the waiting time will be extended to 5 minutes. If the disconnected debater(s) are unable to reconnect within 5 minutes, the disconnected debater(s) will be considered missing. If the disconnected debater(s) haven’t given any statement, the teammate will speak on the disconnected debater’s behalf.
  11. POI can be offered through chat in the debate room, or by quickly turning on the participant's microphone to say “POI” and turn it off again.
  12. After the debate round is finished, participants need to stay in their debate room while the adjudicators are deliberating to give debaters OA/Verbal.
  13. After the OA/Verbal is delivered to debaters, all teams are encouraged to give adjudicators feedback scores based on the OA/Verbal given by the participants’ chair. The adjudicators’ feedback can be given via the tab page.

About this competition

Newscasting is a competition where participants will have the experiences to simulate reporting news, making a live report, conducting a talk show as well as interviewing a special guest. Participants will get the chance to practice and enhance their English and public speaking skills, also the chance to expand their networks. Come and challenge yourself by joining our newscasting competition where you can gain various experiences and enhance your skills!

Rules
  1. Newscasting is an individual competition and a registered participant cannot be replaced.
  2. Participants must use English during the competition. News or talk must not conduct discrimination against SARA nor contain graphic sexual content. Participants must not include any form of profanity.
  3. During the competition, the participant’s devices, internet signals, and connections are the responsibility of each individual participant. If the participants experience unstable connections in the competition, it is not the responsibility of the NEO 2021 committee.
  4. Participants must show their faces and at least half of their body in the video and live round.
  5. For Live rounds, every participant should standby in the chatroom 30 minutes before their performance. The participants must consent the performance to be recorded to avoid any misjudgment from a bad connection.
  6. On the day of the live rounds, participants will be explained the rules by the coordinator inside the chatroom 30 minutes before the competition starts.
  7. During live sessions, every communication device must be silent or turned off.
  8. The participants must not wear any kind of jeans and/or T-shirt during the competition. The participants must wear a neutral or dark-colored blazer, a formal shirt, and formal trousers. Female participants are allowed to wear a formal skirt (advisable to wear a neutral or dark-colored skirt).
  9. Every participant will be called 3 times when their turns come before it is considered a walkout.
  10. Participants are suggested to upload their work 1 day before the due date to avoid any sudden problems with the internet connection or any other situation.
  11. The NEO 2021 committee is not responsible for any interference during the duration of the competition. Interferences include bad internet connection, family or friends intrusion, stray sounds, and many more.
  12. In case of delay, participants are going to be given 5 minutes of tolerance before being disqualified.
  13. In case the participants make mistakes when performing, the NEO 2021 committee does not compromise and/or allow any pauses of duration nor provide any restart for the participants.
  14. All participants should respect and appreciate every judgment from the judges.
  15. All judges’ decisions and results are considered final.
Rounds
  1. Preliminary Round (News Reading) - 25 participants

    Participants have to deliver 2 different news within 2021. They need to recompose the articles into a presentable script. And then perform the news like a professional news anchor.

    1. This round will be a video submission where participants must record themselves performing 2 different news within 2021.
    2. The materials will not be given. Participants must create their own text script for their own video performance in the competition. Participants may use 2 scripts from any source.
    3. News must not contain sensitive issues and/or issues that contain discrimination against a certain group of people.
    4. Participants must upload the video in their provided individual Google Drive Folder with minimum 360p and maximum 720p resolution and MP4 format, with landscape orientation and every participant should only submit once.
    5. Participants must upload the script/notes used to perform the newscasting along with the recorded newscasting video (in a separate file from the video).
    6. The video must be a continuous shot and not be edited in any way.
    7. Performance duration: 5 minutes.
    8. Participants who go over the overtime limit will be disqualified.
  2. Semi-Final (Live Report) - 15 participants

    The best news anchor must have an excellent ability to report news that is live on stage. For this round, participants will be given a specific situation and an article. They will be given 1 interviewee to interview regarding the situation too. Participants have to report the news directly to the audience with their own style.

    1. Participants must perform a live report (explaining the background situation) and interview an interviewee right after.
    2. Specific situations, articles, and details about the interviewee will be given 30 minutes before performing via Zoom.
    3. The participants are allowed to prepare and bring a script for the live report.
    4. Participant's performing order will be randomize.
    5. The materials will be given to each participant privately at different times (based on their performing order).
    6. Each participant will be called 3 times when their turns come before it is considered as a walkout.
    7. Performance duration: 5 minutes + 2 minutes feedback.
    8. Participants who go over the overtime limit will be disqualified.
  3. Final Round (Talk Show) - 5 participants

    In this round, participants will be given the challenge to do a conversation with a special guest with the objective to explore and get information from them. Meanwhile, they should also build a comfortable and conducive atmosphere in the Talk Show.

    1. Participants will be given a topic and background information of the guest, they will then have to explain the topic to the audience then invite the source for a brief Q&A session.
    2. There will be a special guest as an interviewee.
    3. Participants will be given a CV and Resume of the special guest one hour before performing via Zoom.
    4. Participants are expected to create their own talk show based on the CV and Resume.
    5. Scripts are allowed to be brought into the performing room.
    6. Participant's performing order will be randomized.
    7. The materials will be given to each participant privately at different times (based on their performing order).
    8. Each participant will be called 3 times when their turns come before it is considered as a walkout.
    9. Performing time: 8 minutes + 1 minute transition.
    10. Participants who go over the overtime limit will be disqualified.

About this competition

A podcast is a form of audio broadcasting on the web. It can be listened to on the go while commuting to the office or even while working. Most people right now like to do podcasts and talk about anything related to a hot topic. Because of the popularity of podcasts, our National English Olympics has included podcasts as one of the competitions. With this competition, you can explore more about podcasts and challenge yourself to become a good podcaster. What are you waiting for? Join us in the podcast competition!

Rules
  1. The Podcast competition is held as an individual competition.
  2. Participants must mention their full name at the beginning of the recording before starting the podcast.
  3. Podcasts must be done in English. Podcast must not conduct discrimination against SARA nor contain graphic sexual content. Participants must not include any form of profanity.
  4. Participants must submit their work to the NEO 2021 Google Drive that was provided by the committee.
  5. The NEO 2021 committee is not responsible for any bad connection/other interferences during the duration of the competition.
  6. Participant work submission must be an original work that has not been posted elsewhere.
  7. Participants are suggested to upload their work 1 day before the due date to avoid any sudden problems.
  8. Participant works will be submitted to the NEO 2021 Google Drive. Minimum 360p and maximum 720p resolution and MP4 format, with landscape orientation.
  9. Participants must show their faces and at least half of their bodies in the video.
  10. 4 factors are assessed in this podcast. Performance (general appearance, gesture, and stage mastering), Technicality (fluency, pronunciation & grammar, and vocal), Story (flow of the story and story attraction), and Settings (audio, background, and the quality of camera). There will be a deduction of scores when it does not fulfill all the criteria mentioned in the above.
  11. All participants should respect and appreciate every judgment from the judges.
  12. All judges’ decisions and results are considered final.
Rounds
  1. Preliminary

    Participants will be given a fixed topic to base their podcast on and will have to submit their podcast via Google Drive. The topic will be revealed during the Technical Meeting. Participant submissions in this round will be uploaded to Google Drive and will be checked by the judges. The duration of the podcast will be ranging from 10 - 15 minutes.

  2. Semi-final

    The participants choose the theme of the podcast from a selection of themes provided individually. The themes will be announced in the breaking announcement. The duration of the podcast will be ranging from 10 - 15 minutes.

  3. Final

    Participants make their podcast with the theme decided by NEO's committee. The themes will be announced in the breaking announcement. The duration will be ranging from 15 - 20 minutes.

About this competition

Writing a short story is not only about creating a good plot and character in refined words but also the intertextual nature you created in the story itself. Short stories connect us to various worlds and minds, allowing you to convey your ideas into fiction which brings the irreversible moment of life. Are you ready to be the writer who brings your stories to life? Let your imagination guide you! Gain experience in our Short Story Writing Competition for the better change in your own journey now!

Rules
  1. Participants must input the correct contact details in the registered form.
  2. Only one entry per participant will be allowed. The entry must be an original work created by the participant and any kind of plagiarism will not be tolerated or else participants will be disqualified.
  3. Entries should be in English (or English dialect) and participant submission must agree that their work has not been posted or submitted elsewhere.
  4. The story submitted must not contain violence, pornography, and racism. Participants must not include any form of profanity.
  5. Participant works will be submitted to the NEO 2021 Google Forms and stories cannot be altered once entered.
  6. The NEO 2021 committee is not responsible for any bad connection/other interferences during the duration of the competition.
  7. The judges' decisions and results are final and no correspondence will be entered into and all participants should respect and appreciate every outcome that comes from the judges.
Rounds
  1. Preliminary (Free Topic)

    Participants can start submitting from the first time they register. The story should be fiction. It can be in any genre (e.g. romance, thriller, action) and in any perspective (e.g. first person, third person).

    STORY FORMAT REQUIREMENT

    1. One entry per author.
    2. Title Page - The title page of the Short Story must only contain the title of the Short Story and brief synopsis.
    3. Brief Synopsis - The brief synopsis must be a maximum of 100 words. The brief synopsis serves as a description of the Short Story.
    4. Word Limit: MAX. 1500 words (not including the title page).
    5. Size : A4, Font: Times New Roman, 12pt, black.
    6. Double spaced (2.0 line spacing) and margin (Normal).
    7. Insert page number at the footer of the document (except title page), centered.

About this competition

In this field, you are free to express your thoughts and feelings. The power of speech is that you can make an impact with just a few words, even in a virtual world. Boost your self-confidence and freely express yourself by joining our speech field!

Rules
  1. A registered participant can not be replaced.
  2. The speech must be original work. Plagiarism found by or notified to the judges may lead to disqualification.
  3. Participants must use English during the competition. All of the speech shall not contain discrimination against SARA nor contain graphic sexual content. Participants must not include any form of profanity.
  4. Participants must show their faces and at least half of their body in the video and live round.
  5. Participants must wear formal shirts (preferably with ties) with neutral or dark-colored blazers and are not allowed to wear T-shirts.
  6. For the preliminary round, any form of video editing; cutting, dubbing, or subtext, is not allowed.
  7. Participants must upload their work to their own Google Drive and submit the link (view-only) to the form that will be provided.
  8. For the preliminary round, every participant will only be allowed to submit 1 video.
  9. Participants are recommended to upload their work 1 day before the due date to avoid any sudden problems.
  10. For live rounds, there will only be committees, judges, and participants in the room. Spectators are not allowed to join and enter the room during live rounds. Every participant should standby in the chatroom 30 minutes before their performance. The participants must consent the performance to be recorded to avoid any misjudgment from a bad connection.
  11. Every participant will be called 3 times when their turns come before it is considered a walkout.
  12. Participants must prepare the supporting equipment and devices, such as a laptop/PC with a functioning camera and speaker, microphone, earphones (if needed), and a stable internet connection.
  13. The NEO 2021 committee is not responsible for any interference during the duration of the competition. Interferences include bad internet connection, family or friends intrusion, stray sounds, and many more.
  14. In case of delay, participants are going to be given 5 minutes of tolerance by a PIC/committee before being disqualified.
  15. In case the participants make mistakes when performing, the NEO 2021 committee does not compromise and/or allow any pauses of duration nor provide any restart for the participants.
  16. If there are any microphone or audio issues, points will be deducted from the participants as any kind of interference from the participants is not the responsibility of the NEO 2021 committee.
  17. All participants should respect and appreciate every judgment from the judges.
  18. All judges’ decisions and results are considered final.
Rounds
  1. Preliminary (Prepared Speech)
    1. The topic is going to be given at the technical meeting.
    2. The duration of the submitted video should at least be 4 minutes long and a maximum of 6 minutes.
    3. There will be a spare of 30 seconds after the maximum time, there will be no deduction if the participants stopped within the spared 30 seconds, however, after exceeding the 30 seconds mark, the participants will be automatically disqualified.
    4. The participants should submit their script/outline along with the video.
    5. 15 participants with the highest score will go to the semi-final round.
  2. Semi-final (Impromptu Speech)
    1. The semi-final round is going to be held live via the Zoom app (Live Round).
    2. The maximum duration of the speech is 3 minutes.
    3. The participants will be given 15 seconds to prepare before they start.
    4. There will be a spare of 30 seconds after the maximum time, there will be no deduction if the participants stopped within the spared 30 seconds, however, after exceeding the 30 seconds mark, the participants will be automatically disqualified.
    5. The committee will spotlight both the participants and the timekeeper. The timekeeper will show the duration of the speech throughout the speech through a virtual background timer.
    6. 5 participants with the highest score will go to the final round.
  3. Finals (Prepared Speech)
    1. The final round is going to be a live round via the Zoom app.
    2. The topics will be given 1 day before the finals.
    3. The duration of the speech is 8 minutes maximum.
    4. There will be a spare of 30 seconds after the maximum time, there will be no deduction if the participants stopped within the spared 30 seconds, however, after exceeding the 30 seconds mark, the participants will be automatically disqualified.
    5. The committee will spotlight both the participants and timekeeper and the timekeeper will show the duration of the speech throughout the speech through a virtual background timer.
    6. The participants should submit their script/outline before they start (if they have).
    7. The winner of this competition will be determined purely based on the results of this round.

WHAT'S NEW IN NEO 2021

Debate

(v.) a contention by words or arguments.

This year, NEO has decided to add a Debate competition to widen the opportunities for those interested in expressing themselves through comprehensive analysis and battling opinions with others with different points of view.

Debate is a process of proposing arguments between two opposite sides. The two sides are “Pro”, who agrees with the initial statement, and “Con”, who disagrees with the initial statement. The objective of a debate is to decide which side is right from the logical arguments, accompanied by clear and concrete evidence from the debaters.
